The Problem

LLMs excel at drafting creditworthiness summaries and normalizing loan applications. But they have no notion of which jurisdiction's truth-in-lending rules are official, no license to disburse real funds, and no way to verify whether a borrower's debt-to-income ratio actually fits a responsible-lending ceiling. Letting an LLM disburse directly invites fabricated citations, silently irresponsible lending, and liability.

Our Solution

An independent Credit Governor seals the LLM into a single node and wraps it with jurisdiction-aware truth-in-lending checks, fresh affordability verification, and mandatory human approval before any loan disburses. Fabricated citations, incomplete disclosures, debt-to-income violations, or double-disbursement attempts force a hard hold with no override.

What this is not: This system does not hold a lending license itself — you supply the license and underwriting expertise. It also does not model a full underwriting decision (no credit scores, collateral, or payment-history weighting). It provides the compliance scaffold so you don't rebuild the governance layer from scratch.
